Circle of Trust

A circle of trust, previously referred to as an authentication domain, is a federation of any number of service providers (and at least one identity provider) with whom principals can transact business in a secure and apparently seamless environment. To create and populate a circle of trust, you first create an entity to hold the metadata (configuration information that defines a particular identity service architecture) for each provider that will become a member of the authentication domain. Then, you configure and save the authentication domain. Finally, to add an entity (a configured provider) to the authentication domain, you edit the entity's properties.

Note - For more information on entities, see Entities: Provider and Affiliate.
